About

My professional life is a dynamic balance between the **lab bench and the lecture hall**. Currently, I hold a dual role: I'm a **Researcher at Héma-Québec** and an **Associate Professor at Université Laval**.

At Héma-Québec, I get to focus on the really fascinating side of medicine: **hematopoietic stem cells, immunology, and the next steps in clinical cell therapy**. My academic hat at ULaval allows me to teach and mentor the next generation in microbiology and immunology. I love seeing the direct impact of research on public health infrastructure.

My journey has been unique because I combine the scientific rigor of a **Ph.D.** with the strategic operational oversight of an **M.B.A.** This mix allowed me to lead some large, complex public health operations, like supervising the national **Public Cord Blood Bank** and specialized immunohematology labs, ensuring we maintained stringent international standards (Netcord FACT, ISO). It was a blast leading teams dedicated to such high-impact work.

My guiding philosophy is simple and powerful: Give stem cells, milk, blood. Give life.
